Tortillas

Ingredients

  • 10 oz AP Flour (2 Cups plus 2 tablespoons)
  • 3 oz or 1/2 Cup of Lard, Bacon Fat, butter or Veg Shortening, melted
  • 5 oz or 1/2 Cup plus 2 tablespoons of Warm Water around 150ÀöF
  • 1 1/4 teaspoon Baking Powder
  • 1 1/2 teaspoon of Salt

Instructions

  1. Measure out the ingredients and add the dry ingredients into a mixing bowl or stand mixer. Whisk together. Using a stand mixer or food processor, set it to lower and slowly stream in the melted fat until it's absorbed by the flour. Next do the same with the water. Once all the ingredients are in the mixer, increase the speed slightly and allow the dough to knead for 5-10 minutes. Then place in plastic wrap and allow to rest at room temp for 2 hours.
  2. After 2 hours, divide the dough into 8 equal pieces and roll those into balls and then allow those to rest under plastic wrap for 30 more minutes.
  3. After resting, using a rolling pin and a lightly floured surface, roll the dough out as thin as possible. Roll from the center of the dough out and rotate to make sure you maintain a round shape. You want the dough to be paper thin so once you roll it out as thin as you can on the board, tear a few large wax paper sheets and place the rolled out tortilla on to one of the sheets, then place another sheet on top and then continue to roll out the dough a bit more as much as you can, then set aside and repeat with the rest of the dough balls, and continuing to add layers to the wax paper and as you roll the dough with the wax paper, there‚Äôs a bit of compression that happens that helps get the dough sheets to the proper thinness. Then set aside while you preheat a large cast iron pan. If your pan is smaller, flip it upside down and use the bottom of the cast iron to cook the tortillas.
  4. Once hot, add the tortillas one at a time to the cast iron and it will only need to cook for 20-30 seconds if the pan is hot enough. It should instantly start to bubble. After 20-30 seconds, flip and cook the other side for another 20 seconds and then get it out of the pan and into a towel in a pot to steam and continue to soften. Repeat with the rest of the tortillas. If your pan isn‚Äôt hot enough or you cook them too long, the tortillas will dry out and become brittle, making them hard to roll into burritos.
  5. Set aside until ready to use. Store in the fridge in a large ziplock, for up to a week.
